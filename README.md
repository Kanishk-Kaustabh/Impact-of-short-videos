# Impact-of-short-videos
This project analyzes the psychological impact of short-form video platforms like Instagram Reels, Snapchat, and YouTube Shorts on youth. It explores how excessive consumption affects mental health, attention span, self-esteem, and social behavior. The study uses survey data to identify key negative effects and provide actionable recommendations.

## **Overview**

This project examines how short-form video platforms influence the mental and emotional well-being of youth. It highlights the effects of continuous video consumption on anxiety, attention span, self-esteem, social comparison, and addictive behavior. The study is based on primary survey data and offers a detailed understanding of how rapidly changing digital content shapes youth psychology.

## **Objectives**

* To analyze the negative psychological impacts of short video consumption on youth.
* To study patterns related to anxiety, depression, stress, and social comparison.
* To understand how addictive usage affects sleep, focus, and daily life.

## **Methodology**

* **Research Type:** Exploratory
* **Sample Size:** 200+ youth respondents
* **Data Collection:** Structured questionnaire
* **Analysis Tools:** Percentage method, visual charts

## **Key Findings**

* High exposure to short videos increases stress, anxiety, and comparison-based insecurity.
* Over 40% of respondents feel socially disconnected after prolonged usage.
* Many youths report reduced attention span, concentration issues, and disrupted sleep cycles.
* A majority believe short video platforms promote unrealistic beauty and success standards.

## **Conclusion**

Short-form video platforms significantly affect youth mental health, cognitive functioning, and social behavior. The study emphasizes the need for mindful usage, content regulation, and digital wellness practices.
